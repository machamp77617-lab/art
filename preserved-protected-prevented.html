Preserved, Protected, Prevented

Year
2025
Material
64 evidence bags, 26 × 19 cm each, white puzzle pieces

64 is not arbitrary. It is eight squared, the number of squares on a chessboard, and a scale repeatedly standardised within digital systems in a 64-bit architecture, this is how information is segmented, stored, processed. Life itself is rendered computable through such logic: every action, every decision, every moment translated into discrete units that can be recorded, calculated, retrieved.

The jagged edges of a puzzle piece are originally a promise of connection. In this work, that possibility is permanently sealed away by evidence bags. The essence of an evidence bag is irreversible closure. Through the transparent plastic, the audience can see the shape of the fragments but can never touch them.

The most absurd part is that these puzzle pieces are entirely white. Even if the bags were opened and the fragments assembled according to their preset shapes, the result would be a hollow shell. The so-called quantified self does not culminate in a more complete life, but rather a structurally intact, white, ghost-like existence.
